About Me

Growing up, I experienced the monsoon not as data, but as a rhythm you could feel—in the air, the soil, and the silence before rain. Today, as a PhD researcher at the Centre for Atmospheric Sciences, IIT Delhi, I study that same monsoon through the lens of machine learning and atmospheric physics.

My research focuses on physics-guided neural networks to forecast the Indian Summer Monsoon’s active and break phases. I explore deep learning architectures like transformers and Fourier Neural Operators to model the monsoon’s complex, multi-scale dynamics.

A central goal of my work is to better understand interactions between monsoon intraseasonal oscillations and climate drivers like ENSO. By embedding physical constraints into models, I aim to improve accuracy, interpretability, and efficiency—delivering forecasts valuable for agriculture and water planning.

Beyond research, I’m drawn to literature, politics, and cinema.
